nbkc Bank

This is where I currently work and have been since May 2018. Nbkc Bank is a tranditional bank that also offers excellent mortgage services all over the United State. My first position was called Origination Support Specialist (OSS) which is just a fancy title for a Loan Officer assistant. Last May, I transfered into the processing department and I have been working as a loan processor ever since. It is a pretty fun place to work. The hours are great, the pay is solid, and the work itself isn't too stressful. I think that it is the perfect place to work while I train to become a Software Engineer.

Michelle O'Connor Real Estate

This is my mom's real estate company that she has had for 10+ years. While I technically only worked here part-time from January 2017 - April 2018, my entire life was spent in some shape or form at the office with my parents. I started learning how to do real estate work when I was 10 years old! There were pros and cons to working with my mom, but ultimately it didn't work out and I left for my current position at nbkc Bank.

Liberty Public Schools

Before going full time at nbkc Bank, I was working in the high school band sector. Here, I was a part time Assistant Band Director who primarly worked with the high school drumline. This was a really fun position as I was in charge of writing the music, the warmups, and the technique packet. The hours were great - again, it was part time so I worked about 10-15 hours a week here while also working at Michelle O'Connor Real Estate and Blue Springs High School.

Blue Springs Public Schools

As mentioned above, I was working at Blue Springs High School while simultaneously working at Liberty Public Schools and Michelle O'Connor Real Estate. Here my title was Snare Technician and I only work with the high school drumline. Unlike my position at Liberty North, I didn't write any music or warmups. I was given the material and worked on it with the students. Blue Springs has a reputation for having a very competitive marching band program so they already had those things taken care of. This was a great job and I am still close with the staff here. In fact, they were just shown on TV at the Macy's Day Parade in New York City!

Daras Gas Station

However, it was a college job and they let me do my homework while I was working. The only worthwhile story from this position was during the billion dollar Powerball from a few years back. I had multiple professors come in and drop hundreds of dollars on lottery tickets promising me that if they won, they would give me a million bucks. Sadly though, not a single one of them won and I am still not a millionaire.

Chick Fil A

This was another college job that I worked. Without a doubt, this was the hardest job I have ever had or will ever have. I worked in the kitchen as a fry cook and it was miserable. I ultimately worked here for about 7 months. I stayed that long because the pay was good and I got free food. If I was able to get free food while I was at work, it was a huge plus to me!

Chipotle

This was the job I held the longest while in college. I believe I was there was from Fall 2012 - Spring 2014. Honestly, I only stayed there that long because getting free Chipotle was (at the time) unbeatable. This job was not bad and I am still friends with some of the people that I worked with. Also, yes, I can still fold burritos like a champ.

Menards

This was a random job to say the least. The only reason I got it was because my roommate at the time was working here and he helped me get the position. I worked in flooring and carpets and, funnily enough, I knew absolutely nothing about either of those things! Either way, this job was alright. It conflicted with my school schedule ultimately and I ended up leaving for the greener pastures of Chipotle.
